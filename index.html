The AquaSentry Mandate

Addressing the critical health and economic risks associated with unchecked community water sources.

1. What We Solve: The Monitoring Gap

Current State: Blind Reliance

Many **rural and peri-urban communities** rely entirely on local water infrastructure—be it **handpumps, borewells, or local tap outlets**—with a critical absence of local, **real-time water safety verification**. Testing, when it occurs, is typically centralized, requiring samples to be sent to **distant, costly labs**.

This lack of immediate feedback creates a window of vulnerability, transforming essential water sources into potential health hazards.

Concrete Harms to Community and Economy

Direct Health Crisis

Immediate waterborne diseases (e.g., cholera, typhoid) and **long-term toxin exposure** (arsenic, fluoride, lead) leading to chronic illness.

Financial Burden

Increased **household spending** on medical care, the purchase of bottled or treated water, and financial loss from **lost work or school days**.

The core necessity: **Early detection prevents illness and saves money.** Local, cheap, and easy-to-read devices empower both communities and local maintenance teams to act instantly.

2. Analyzing the Root Causes

Source Contamination

Industrial effluent and agricultural runoff are the primary external sources that contaminate groundwater and surface water bodies.

Aging Infrastructure

Contamination within the distribution system due to aging or corroded pipes and tank contamination.

Lack of Local Testing

Insufficient local testing infrastructure; labs are costly and geographically distant.

Operational Hurdles

Low user awareness, irregular maintenance of existing devices/infrastructure, and critical **power access issues** at remote sites.

AquaSentry directly addresses these root causes by providing autonomous, actionable data where it is needed most.
